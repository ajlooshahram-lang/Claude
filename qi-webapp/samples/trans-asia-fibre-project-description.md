## 1. Overview
We are deploying a new submarine fibre optic cable system linking eight markets
across Asia and the Western Pacific. The system provides a high-capacity backbone
for cloud, mobile backhaul and international transit traffic. The programme value
is approximately USD 1.3 billion and the planned duration is 60 months from award
to ready-for-service.

The trunk route is about 9,500 km with branching units to each landing country.
The system carries 24 fibre pairs on the trunk with an initial design capacity of
around 18 Tbps per fibre pair, using modern coherent transmission and ROADM-based
branching.

## 2. Landing countries and stations
The cable lands in eight countries. Each landing requires a cable landing station,
a beach manhole, terrestrial backhaul to a major data centre, and the relevant
national landing licence:

- **Indonesia** — landing near Batam, backhaul to Jakarta.
- **Malaysia** — landing near Mersing, backhaul to Kuala Lumpur.
- **Brunei** — landing near Tutong, backhaul to Bandar Seri Begawan.
- **Vietnam** — landing near Da Nang, backhaul to Ho Chi Minh City.
- **Thailand** — landing near Songkhla, backhaul to Bangkok.
- **Philippines** — landing near Batangas, backhaul to Manila.
- **Taiwan** — landing near Tamsui (Taipei), backhaul to the Taipei metro.
- **Guam** — landing near Piti, interconnect to the Pacific hub.

## 3. Scope of work
- Marine route survey, desktop study and permitting across all eight territories.
- Submarine cable and repeater supply, marine installation and burial in shallow water.
- Cable landing stations: civil works, power, DC plant, SLTE and network management.
- Terrestrial backhaul to each anchor data centre.
- System integration, commissioning, end-to-end testing (OTDR, BER, capacity) and
  acceptance to ready-for-service.

## 4. Key constraints and risks
- Multi-country permitting and cable landing licences on the critical path.
- Marine seasons: typhoon season in the Philippines, Taiwan and Guam; monsoon in
  the Indonesia/Malaysia/Brunei waters.
- Territorial sensitivities in the South China Sea affecting route and survey.
- Limited cable-ship availability; long-lead repeaters and trunk cable.
- Environmental approvals near coral reefs and protected coastal zones.

## 5. Stakeholders
- Owning consortium (investors and anchor customers).
- National telecom regulators and landing-party operators in each country.
- Marine installation contractor and cable supplier.
- Lenders' technical advisor and an independent environmental consultant.

## 6. Objectives / definition of done
- All eight landing licences secured and stations accepted.
- End-to-end system tested and accepted at the contracted capacity.
- Handover to operations with as-built documentation and spares.
- Within the USD 1.3 billion budget and the 60-month schedule.
