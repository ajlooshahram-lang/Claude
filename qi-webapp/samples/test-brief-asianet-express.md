# AsiaNet Express — Submarine Cable Project Description

## What we are building
AsiaNet Express is a submarine fibre optic cable system connecting eight countries
across Southeast Asia and the Western Pacific. It provides high-capacity backbone
connectivity for cloud platforms, 5G mobile backhaul, and wholesale transit.

Budget: approximately USD 1.3 billion. Duration: 60 months to ready-for-service.
Route: about 9,500 km trunk with 24 fibre pairs, coherent transmission, ROADM
branching units.

## Landing countries
- Indonesia — landing near Jakarta, backhaul to Equinix JK1.
- Malaysia — landing near Mersing, backhaul to Cyberjaya.
- Brunei — landing near Tutong, backhaul to BSB.
- Vietnam — landing near Da Nang, backhaul to HCMC.
- Thailand — landing near Songkhla, backhaul to Bangkok.
- Philippines — landing near Batangas, backhaul to Manila.
- Taiwan — landing near Tamsui, backhaul to Taipei.
- Guam — landing near Piti, Pacific interconnect hub.

## Scope
Marine route survey across all eight territories. Submarine cable and repeater
supply. Marine installation and shallow-water burial. Eight cable landing stations
with civil works, power, SLTE and network management. Terrestrial backhaul.
System integration, commissioning, OTDR/BER/capacity testing, and acceptance.

## Key risks
Multi-country permitting (9–14 months per country). Typhoon season in the
Philippines, Taiwan and Guam. Monsoon in Indonesia/Malaysia/Brunei waters.
South China Sea territorial sensitivities. Limited cable-ship availability.
Environmental approvals near coral reefs.

## Stakeholders
Investor consortium. National telecom regulators in all 8 countries. Marine
installation contractor. Submarine cable supplier. Lenders' technical advisor.
