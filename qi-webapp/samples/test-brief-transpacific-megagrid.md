## 1. Programme overview
APEX-1 is a USD 1.3 billion, 60-month submarine fibre optic cable mega-grid
connecting eight markets across Asia and the Western Pacific. Unlike a single
trunk, APEX-1 is a self-healing **mesh**: every landing is reachable by at least
two diverse paths, so a single cable cut cannot isolate any country. The system
backs cloud regions, subsea-to-data-centre routes, mobile backhaul, government
traffic and wholesale capacity sold as IRUs to anchor customers.

Headline numbers: ~12,800 km of submarine cable across the mesh, 24 fibre pairs
on the trunks and 12–16 on the branches, coherent transmission with an initial
design capacity of ~20 Tbps per fibre pair, ROADM branching units, and ~190
repeaters. Ready-for-service is contracted for month 60, with two of the eight
countries lit early at month 30 as a phase-1 revenue milestone.

## 2. The eight landing countries (mesh topology)
Each country requires a primary cable landing station, most require a diverse
second landing, plus beach manholes, terrestrial backhaul to an anchor data
centre, and that country's national cable-landing licence:

- **Indonesia** — primary landing near Batam (backhaul to Jakarta), diverse landing on the north Java coast.
- **Malaysia** — landing near Mersing (backhaul to Kuala Lumpur), with a Sarawak/Sabah branch.
- **Brunei** — landing near Tutong (backhaul to Bandar Seri Begawan).
- **Vietnam** — landings near Da Nang and Quy Nhon (backhaul to Ho Chi Minh City).
- **Thailand** — landing near Songkhla (backhaul to Bangkok).
- **Philippines** — landings near Batangas and on Luzon (backhaul to Manila).
- **Taiwan** — landing near Tamsui (Taipei) with a diverse east-coast landing.
- **Guam** — landing near Piti (Mariana hub, US jurisdiction, interconnect to trans-Pacific systems).

## 3. Scope of work (multi-discipline, multi-campaign)
- Desktop study, marine route survey (three separate marine campaigns across two weather windows), and multi-country permitting.
- Submarine cable, repeaters and ROADM branching-unit supply; marine installation, burial in shallow water, and rock-berm/articulated-pipe protection across fishing grounds and shipping lanes.
- Eight-to-twelve cable landing stations: civil works, power and DC plant, SLTE, network management and security.
- Terrestrial backhaul to each anchor data centre, including diverse duct routes.
- System integration, FAT/SAT, ITP-governed inspections, NCR workflow, end-to-end testing (OTDR, BER, capacity) and staged acceptance to ready-for-service.

## 4. Commercial, financing & contracts
- Owning consortium of five investors plus two anchor cloud customers; capacity pre-sold as IRUs to fund construction.
- Senior debt from a lender club; monthly drawdowns tied to verified progress (earned value), retention and milestone certificates.
- Multi-currency exposure: USD base with local-currency civil works in all eight countries; FX hedging required.
- Main marine contract under FIDIC; landing-station civils under NEC4 with an active variation/claims register and liquidated damages for late RFS.

## 5. Key constraints and risks
- Multi-country permitting and cable-landing licences are the binding critical path; several run 9–14 months.
- Marine weather: typhoon season around the Philippines, Taiwan and Guam; monsoon across the Indonesia, Malaysia and Brunei waters — only narrow installation windows.
- Geopolitics: South China Sea territorial sensitivities affecting survey and route; Taiwan Strait tension affecting east-Asia legs; cross-border landing-rights diplomacy.
- Seismic and deep-trench hazards near Taiwan, the Philippine Trench and the Mariana region.
- Environmental approvals near coral reefs, seagrass and marine protected areas; EIA in every jurisdiction.
- Long-lead repeaters and trunk cable; limited global cable-ship availability shared with competing systems.
- Single-operator landing-party dependency in at least one market, creating commercial leverage risk.

## 6. Stakeholders
- Owning consortium (five investors + two anchor customers) and the programme management office.
- National telecom regulators and licensed landing-party operators in all eight countries.
- Marine installation contractor, submarine cable supplier and repeater vendor.
- Lenders' technical advisor, independent environmental consultant, and an insurance/marine-warranty surveyor.

## 7. Objectives / definition of done
- All eight national cable-landing licences secured; all landing stations accepted.
- Phase-1: two countries lit and revenue-generating by month 30.
- Full mesh end-to-end tested and accepted at contracted capacity, with diverse-path protection proven.
- Handover to operations with as-built records, spares and a 24/7 NOC.
- Delivered within the USD 1.3 billion budget and the 60-month schedule, with lender and regulatory sign-off in every jurisdiction.
